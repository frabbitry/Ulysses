The Ulysses Project

With the Ulysses Project, we are attempting to examine how James Joyce recreated the city of Dublin in Ulysses. As the New York Times chronicles, “Joyce knew and remembered Dublin in such detail that he bragged (and that’s the word) that if the city were destroyed, it could be rebuilt in its entirety from his written works.” Ulysses, through the story of Leopold Bloom’s walk through the city on June 16, 1904, uses vivid detail to paint the city. However, Ulysses did not only recreate the city of Dublin; it attempted to retell The Odyssey, which is one of the reasons why Ulysses has stood the test of time as a hallmark of Modernist literature. The book in and of itself is a literary allusion. Therefore, this project looks at how Joyce recreated Dublin through the use of literary allusions and references. We examine the allusions and references Joyce uses at each of the book’s settings to try and gain a better understanding of how those allusions were used to build his Dublin.

As such, we have attempted to pinpoint the settings included in Ulysses and the references included within them to see how they were related. We used XML to tag several chapters of Ulysses, including “Telemachus,” "Nestor,” “Proteus,” “Calypso,” “Cyclops,” “Wandering Rocks,” “Lotus Eaters,” “Eumaeus,” “Ithaca,” and “Penelope.” We then used QGIS to map the settings included within those sections and create heatmaps based on allusions used most frequently, and we also used Cytoscape to create network graphs showing, perhaps more explicitly, the relationships between specific locations and allusions.

While we believe that these graphical representations of Joyce’s Dublin are interesting, they are incomplete. Because of time limitations, we weren’t able to encode and work with the whole novel, only the sections listed above. Moreover, many of the specific locations that existed in 1904 are no longer present, and so our location data is sometimes inaccurate because we had to make educated guesses about where those places are now. It is also very possible that we incorrectly coded or neglected to code certain references or allusions. Nevertheless, we believe that our project provides an interesting glimpse into the way that Joyce recreated Dublin through allusions in Ulysses. We hope that you enjoy the project website and find our results interesting.

This project is being done through the Digital Humanities: Coding and Visualization class at the University of Pittsburgh at Greensburg.

The project team members are Fiona, Kiara, and Brad.
